Cool stuff

1) Sand deposition and plant establishment: Time series of Landsat data can capture some land dynamics that would be missed if we only analyzed individual images at a time. Here we can see how seasonal flooding and drying of a river margin ends up in enough sand deposition that the flooding stops over time. Eventually vegetation starts to grow. Timeseries shows the first short wave infrared band.
2) Vegetation regrowth in the Colombian Amazon: The slopes of the fitted segments in the time series can be mapped to reveal areas of regrowing vegetation. The RGB combination shows the slopes of the SWIR1 (Red), NIR (Green), and SWIR2 (Blue) for segments intersecting the year 2010. A two standard deviations stretch was applied to enhance the contrast. Vegetation regrowth appears bright green due to negative slopes in the SWIR bands, and positive in the NIR. The panel at the bottom shows the SWIR1 time series for an example pixel with ongoing forest regeneration, and is marked with a crossmark in the image.
3) Flooding in the Colombian Amazon: Amplitudes of the sinusoid in the time series segments can be used to reveal areas with seasonal flooding, like this areas in the Eastern Colombian Amazon. The RGB combination shows the annual amplitudes of the NIR (Red), SWIR1 (Green), and Red (Blue) bands. A two standard deviations stretch was applied to enhance the contrast. Locations with clear seasonal flooding events appear white and yellow in the image. Many of these places correspond to natural grasslands and appear as light green. The panel at the bottom shows the SWIR1 time series for an example pixel marked with a crossmark in the image.
